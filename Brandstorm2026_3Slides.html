What Science
Already Knows
Established physics — no experiments needed to verify this part.
01
Eddy Currents Alternating magnetic fields induce "eddy currents" in conductive materials — including moist human tissue. This is proven physics used in MRI and industrial NDT sensors.
02
Depth vs. Frequency Higher frequency = shallower penetration (δ = 1/√πfμσ). At ~100 MHz, the field stays in the stratum corneum. At ~1 MHz, it reaches the dermis.
03
Skin Moisture = Fragrance Duration Peer-reviewed literature confirms that skin hydration directly affects fragrance evaporation rate and scent character. Dry skin loses fragrance faster.
Our Concept
A dual-frequency, non-contact sensor — designed around these physics principles.
What We Plan
to Prove
Our roadmap from concept to validated prototype.
1
Build the PoC Assemble a working sensor (eddy current + distance + temperature modules) for under €30.
2
Validate on Volunteers Measure 20+ participants across varying skin types, hydration levels, and seasons. Compare against reference measurements.
3
Build the Fragrance Link Measure skin before and after fragrance application. Correlate skin moisture readings with fragrance longevity across a range of L'Oréal Luxe scents.
4
Train the AI Model Use collected data to train a personalization model — recommending fragrance concentration and application points based on individual skin profiles.
"We believe eddy current physics, applied to skin moisture at two frequencies, can predict fragrance performance with accuracy comparable to clinical devices — at a fraction of the cost."

Zero Consumables by Design
No probe covers, no gel pads, no wipes — ever. Non-contact is our core principle, not an afterthought. Eliminates 100% of single-use waste at the point of sale.

Right Product, First Time
We believe personalized recommendations will cut fragrance returns. Fewer returns means less packaging waste, less transport emissions, less unsold inventory.

Our zero-consumable design directly supports L'Oréal's 2030 commitment to eliminate plastic from point-of-sale materials.

Inclusive by Nature
Non-contact sensors are inherently unbiased across skin tones, skin conditions, and cultural backgrounds. No calibration changes per skin type needed.
