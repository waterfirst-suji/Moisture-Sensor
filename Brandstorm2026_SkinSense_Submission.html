🔬 Slide 2 of 3  ·  The Innovation

How SkinSense Works

The Technology — Explained for Everyone

🏔️
Intuitive Analogy #1 — How It Senses
"Like throwing a stone into a lake to measure its depth."
SkinSense sends an invisible micro-wave (magnetic field) toward the skin — exactly like the technology inside your wireless phone charger or subway card — completely safe and non-ionizing. The wetter the skin, the more the wave "bounces back" differently. We read that difference and calculate moisture content — without ever touching you.
🌊
Intuitive Analogy #2 — The Two-Frequency Strategy
"Sending both a shallow ripple and a deep wave simultaneously."
Existing sensors only read the skin surface. SkinSense fires two different frequencies at once: a fast, shallow wave (100 MHz) reads surface hydration — how your top notes will perform; a slow, deep wave (1 MHz) reads deep dermal moisture — how your base notes will last. This gives L'Oréal clients a complete hydration portrait, not just a snapshot.
🧠
Intuitive Analogy #3 — AI Correction
"A smart sensor that ignores the weather and focuses on your skin."
Non-contact sensors are vulnerable to distance variation and ambient temperature. SkinSense's onboard AI continuously monitors the gap between device and skin plus room temperature — and auto-corrects in real time. Whether measured in a boutique in Paris, at 38°C in Dubai, or during a home skincare routine, it always delivers lab-grade accuracy.

The SkinSense × Luxury Fragrance Engine

Step 1  ·  SCAN
Customer holds wrist near device — SkinSense measures surface moisture (stratum corneum) and deep moisture (dermis) in 3 seconds. No probes. No gels. No contact.

Step 2  ·  ANALYZE
AI processes skin data: hydration level · moisture gradient (surface vs. deep) · skin temperature · individual profile (if returning customer). Predicts fragrance longevity for each note family and calculates optimal concentration (EdT / EdP / Extrait).

Step 3  ·  RECOMMEND
The system surfaces a ranked list of fragrances from L'Oréal Luxe's portfolio (YSL, Lancôme, Armani, Maison Margiela…) with predicted longevity, ideal application points, and a layering ritual — personalized to that person's skin, that day's weather, and their fragrance history.

Business Model, Sustainability & Scale

Customer Journey

① ARRIVE
Customer walks into a L'Oréal Luxe boutique. SA invites them to try the SkinSense experience — positioned as a premium ritual, not a clinical test.
② SCAN
In 3 seconds, wrist or forearm is measured. A beautiful digital display reveals their personal "Hydration Portrait" — surface and deep moisture, skin temperature.
③ DISCOVER
AI generates a personalized fragrance shortlist from the brand portfolio, with predicted longevity (e.g., "This Extrait will last 9 hrs on your skin today").
④ RETURN
Customer profile saved (with consent). App tracks seasonal skin changes. Automated reminders: "Your skin is drier this winter — your fragrance may last shorter. Re-test?"

Revenue Model

B2B — Hardware

Retail-grade SkinSense devices sold/leased to L'Oréal Luxe boutiques and department store counters worldwide. Target: 5,000 counters in Year 2.

B2B — Data & SaaS

Anonymized, aggregated skin moisture trends by region, season, and demographics — sold as a research subscription to L'Oréal R&I for product formulation insights.

B2C — Personal Device

Consumer-grade SkinSense + app (BLE). Monthly subscription for personalized fragrance & skincare rituals. Target price: €99 device + €9.99/mo premium tier.

🌿 Sustainability & L'Oréal for the Future

Zero Consumables

Traditional skin sensors use disposable gel pads, alcohol wipes, and single-use probe covers. SkinSense requires none. The non-contact design eliminates 100% of consumable waste — directly aligned with L'Oréal's "L'Oréal for the Future" zero-plastic and zero-waste commitments.

Precision Reduces Waste

Personalized fragrance recommendations mean customers buy the right product first time, reducing returns and unsold inventory. Fewer returns = less packaging waste, less logistics carbon. A single percentage point reduction in L'Oréal Luxe's return rate represents millions of bottles saved annually.

01
Judgment — What guided your decision-making in complex situations?
Our toughest decision was choosing between a cheap, simple single-frequency sensor and a complex dual-frequency system. The single-frequency approach would have been faster to build, but it could only measure surface moisture — it would miss the "deep hydration" story entirely. We consulted our mentor — a senior researcher at Samsung Display with 130+ US patents — who confirmed: the dual-frequency approach was technically harder, but it was the only way to create something that couldn't be easily replicated. We chose the harder path because we believe genuine innovation requires genuine depth.
02
Resilience — What hurdles did you face and how did you overcome them?
We initially ordered a NI USB-6501 data acquisition module — ₩58,800 we had saved up — only to discover after arrival that it was a digital I/O board, unable to read analog sensor signals at all. A painful and expensive mistake. Instead of giving up, we rearchitected the entire system around an Arduino Nano and a WL02 eddy current sensor — a solution that turned out to be not only cheaper, but more accurate and more portable than our original design. That setback became the foundation of our competitive advantage.
03
Ambition — What is your vision and long-term goal?
In twelve months, we want SkinSense sensors running in ten luxury fragrance counters. In five years, we envision every L'Oréal boutique globally offering a 30-second "skin signature" scan that predicts exactly which fragrance will last longest on your skin, smell truest to your chemistry, and evolve most beautifully through the day. Our ultimate ambition: turn skin data into the new universal language of beauty personalization — starting with fragrance, expanding to skincare, cosmetics, and beyond.
04
Empathy — How do you support each other as a team?
We have a rule: no one faces a technical wall alone for more than 24 hours. When our AI model's correction accuracy dropped below acceptable levels during late-night testing, [Name 2] shared the problem in our group chat at 2 AM — by morning, [Name 1] had proposed a sensor fusion approach that fixed it. We genuinely come from three different disciplines — physics, computer science, and business — and we've learned that our strongest ideas emerge exactly at the intersections of those three worlds. Empathy for each other's expertise makes us a better team than any one of us alone.
05
Learning Agility — How did you handle unfamiliar topics?
None of us had a background in dermatology or luxury fragrance. We started by reading academic papers on skin impedance published in the Journal of Investigative Dermatology — and realized that skin moisture is, in fact, the least-studied variable in the entire fragrance purchase journey. We then interviewed three cosmetics science students, watched a L'Oréal Luxe counter experience, and mapped the customer journey ourselves. Every topic we didn't know became a reason to ask more questions — not a reason to stop. That's how three engineering students ended up understanding the luxury fragrance market well enough to try to reshape it.
